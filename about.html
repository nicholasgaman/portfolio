About Me

I have always been driven by a fundamental curiosity to understand how things work. This internal drive to understand and expand my knowledge is what led me to my current studies in Mechatronic Engineering.

Prior to commencing my degree, I worked assembling battery management systems at EV Power in Margaret River. It was there that I developed a deep appreciation for electronics and programming. That hands-on experience in repairing, assembling, and testing hardware sparked a genuine passion that I felt compelled to pursue further.

I chose Mechatronics because it represents the perfect overlap of robotics, automation, electronics, and programming, all areas that I find equally fascinating. Over the course of my degree, this interest has only intensified. I have included a selection of my favorite university projects here to illustrate the technical learning and problem-solving experience I have gained.

As I approach the end of my studies, I am eager to transition into the professional world. Having thoroughly enjoyed every facet of the discipline, I remain open to a diverse range of engineering opportunities. My primary goal is to apply my learning and experience to meaningful, real-world challenges.
